# HEIC to JPG: How to Open iPhone Photos Anywhere

Since iOS 11, iPhones save photos as **HEIC** (High Efficiency Image Container) by default. It stores the same quality as JPEG at roughly half the size — great for your phone's storage, annoying everywhere else: Windows may not preview it, many web forms reject it, older software can't open it, and Android support is inconsistent.

## Option 1: Convert in the browser (no install, private)

The fastest fix when you already have the HEIC file on any device:

1. Open the free [HEIC to JPG converter](https://dengfan.top/heic-to-jpg.html).
2. Drop in one or more .heic files.
3. Download the JPGs.

Conversion happens locally in your browser — files are never uploaded to a server, which matters if the photos are personal. Need transparency or lossless output instead? Use [HEIC to PNG](https://dengfan.top/heic-to-png.html); for the smallest web-ready files, [HEIC to WebP](https://dengfan.top/heic-to-webp.html).

## Option 2: Stop the problem at the source (iPhone setting)

If you constantly share photos to non-Apple devices:

**Settings → Camera → Formats → Most Compatible** — the camera will shoot JPEG from now on. Trade-off: photos take ~2× the storage, and you lose some computational-photography benefits. Existing HEIC photos stay HEIC.

A lighter-touch alternative: **Settings → Photos → Transfer to Mac or PC → Automatic**, which converts to JPEG only when transferring over USB, keeping HEIC on the phone.

## Option 3: Share instead of transfer

When you AirDrop to a Mac or share via most messaging apps, iOS often converts to JPEG automatically. Emailing a photo to yourself also usually delivers a JPEG. Slow for batches, fine for one-offs.

## Which should you pick?

| Situation | Best route |
|---|---|
| One-off file someone sent you | Browser converter (Option 1) |
| Batch of files on a PC | Browser converter (Option 1) |
| You share to Windows/Android weekly | Camera setting (Option 2) |
| Single photo from your own phone | Share sheet (Option 3) |

## After converting: check the size

JPEGs from HEIC can double in size. If a form has an upload limit, compress the result to a target like [100KB](https://dengfan.top/compress-100kb.html) or [200KB](https://dengfan.top/compress-200kb.html) after conversion.
